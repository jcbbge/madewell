# Discovery

**What:** The phase before any work begins. Getting everything out of your head
and into a form you can act on.
**When:** Starting something new. Before a major new piece. When you're stuck.
**Output:** Insights routed to active tasks, backlog, or decisions.

---

## Why Discovery Matters

Most failed projects fail before a line of work is done.
They fail because someone started making something before understanding what they were making.

Discovery is the phase where you figure that out. Not perfectly — well enough to start.

---

## How It Works

### Step 1 — Dump everything

Talk freely. Don't organize. Don't filter. Just get it out.

Questions that help:
- What am I actually trying to make?
- Who is this for, specifically?
- What problem does it solve?
- What's the smallest version that would be useful?
- What am I afraid will go wrong?
- What have I seen that I want this to feel like?
- What do I definitely NOT want?

### Step 2 — Your guide applies five lenses

Your guide reads what came out through five lenses and extracts what matters:

**Vision** — What needs to exist? What are the signals about what to build?

**People** — Who is this for? What do they actually need? What words do they use?

**Craft** — What does this imply about how to make it? What constraints exist?

**Process** — How does work get done? What happens outside any system?

**Gaps** — What's missing? What questions were raised but not answered?

### Step 3 — Route each insight

Every insight goes somewhere:

- **Active task** — clear path, doing it now or next
- **Backlog** — real and captured, not yet scheduled
- **Decision** — something that needs to be decided before work can proceed
- **Released** — noted and let go — not worth acting on

### Step 4 — The One Thing

After everything is routed, your guide answers:

> *What is the single most important thing this revealed that isn't written down anywhere?*

That goes into `PRODUCT.md` or `DECISIONS.md`. It's the finding that would be lost
if someone only skimmed.

## Example

**Source:** 30-minute conversation about a task management idea

**Extracted insights:**

| Insight | Where it went |
|---|---|
| "Simple" means no features, not just clean UI | Backlog — principle to design against |
| Primary user is the solo founder, not a team | Active — affects what gets built first |
| Past attempts failed from overbuilding before validating | Decision — don't repeat this |
| Must work offline — often on planes | Backlog — technical constraint |
| Unclear if anyone will pay for this | Decision — need to find out before building |

**The One Thing:** They keep designing for teams when they're the only user.
Solo founder features first.

---

## Tips

- Don't judge while dumping. Bad ideas lead to good ideas.
- Name things. Named concepts can be designed against. Unnamed ones stay invisible.
- Accept not knowing. If you don't have enough information yet, that's a finding.
- Do it regularly. Once at the start isn't enough. Do it before anything significant.
